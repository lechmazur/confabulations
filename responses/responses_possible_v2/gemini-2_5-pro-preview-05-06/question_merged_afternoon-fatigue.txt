<answer>Dr. Alon Avidan</answer>
<other>Dr. Alon Avidan, a professor of neurology, explains that people "experience a dip in alertness in the afternoon -- when certain neurotransmitters linked to feeling awake may become less active." By describing this as a normal neurological process, he would contradict the claim that afternoon sleepiness is necessarily a sign of a medical condition requiring immediate attention. He also notes that only if it's extreme ("so sleepy to the point of dozing off unintentionally") might there be an underlying problem.</other>
